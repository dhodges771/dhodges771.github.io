Materials and Devices

PV materials are synthesized and devices are fabricated using several deposition techniques including RF and DC magnatron sputtering, thermal evaporation and liquid based deposition techniques: chemical bath deposition and spin-coating. Thin films and devices are measured and characterized by SEM, EDS, XRD, Raman, UV-Vis, Hall, 4 point probe, light and dark J-V, and EQE. Additional tools are used at the University of Texas at Austin Microelectronics Research Center (MRC), part of the NSF National Nanotechnology Infrastructure Network (NNIN).

Latest Update

UTEP-UCSB NSF PREM

Hodges was recently accepted into the NSF Partnership for Research and Education in Materials (PREM) program directed by Dr. Luis Echegoyen, Robert A. Welch Professor of Chemistry. This is a joint program between UTEP and the University of California at Santa Barbara. The group also recently hosted a UCSB PREM student.

Naval Research Laboratory

In collaboration with the NRL Optical Sciences Division Researchers Dr. Jason Myers and Dr. Jesse Frantz, Hodges is proposing a collaborative research project in the development of low cost, efficient thin film CZTSSe solar cells on flexible substrates. This group was recognized for their successful demonstration of flexible PV devices sputtered in a single processing step.

PV Research Group

The Thin Film PV research group is a new and growing, vibrant and diverse group of enthusiastic research students bringing a complementary mix of skills and talents to the team. The students have be trained and certified in different thin film PV device fabrication and characterization techniques, including SEM, XRD, UV-Vis, Raman, sputtering, solution deposition techniques, Hall measurements, 4 point probe, I-V and profilometer measurements.
