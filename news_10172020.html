International Travel to the United Kingdom  

Toured Imperial College London, the Department of Bioengineering - hosted by Marshall Scholar Ophelia Johnson. Toured the University of Oxford and Professor Henry Snaith's Photovoltaic and Optoelectronic Device Group at the Clarendon Laboratory - hosted by Marshall Scholar Kelly Schutt.

Brookhaven National Lab

NSF LSAMP and Summer Research at BNL

National Synchrotron Light Source II (NSLS II) new user. Used the SRX 5-ID beamline for XRF and XANES. CFN SEM/FIB new user. 2017 NSF LSAMP Internships- three of our students were mentored by BNL Scientists on different projects. Dr. Aleksey Bolotnikov mentored Jazmin Munoz in the Fabrication of Virtual Frisch-Grid Detectors for Large Area CdZnTe Arrays. Dr. Anwar Hossain mentored Robert Sifuentes in the Detector Development and Performance Evaluation of Cadmium Zinc Telluride Detectors. Dr. Andrei Nomerotski mentored Jenny Lopez in Optical Photon Counting and Phosphorescence Lifetime Imaging with the TimPixCam. Collaboration with BNL Scientists on cadmium zinc telluride (CZT) X-ray and gamma-ray radiation detection and Compton Imaging through the the Visiting Faculty and NSF LSAMP programs. Collaboration with the Nonprofileration and National Security group, and with the University of Michigan's UMImaging group. Collaboration also with Scientists at the Center for Functional Nanomaterials (CFN) in probing perovskites using PL, XPS, SEM/FIB and AFM techniques.

NREL

Participation in NREL's Hands-On Photovoltaics Experience (HOPE) program, learning from NREL's Scientists. Participation in NREL's Faculty Development workshop learning new approaches and best practices in teaching PV from leaders in the PV community.

El Paso Electric Company

The Renewable and Emergent Technologies Team at El Paso Electric Company provided tours of their newest Community Solar facility and the Stanton Tower Solar System to a large group of students from our Applied Photovoltaics, Renewable Energy and Energy Sustainabilty courses. The El Paso Electric team also gave a state-of-art presentation about the company's renewable programs and projects. The Community Solar facility is an impressive 3 MW facility with First Solar Thin Film CdTe single axis tracking panels. The El Paso Electric Community Solar program is 100% subscribed.
