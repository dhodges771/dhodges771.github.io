PV Device Fabrication

PV device layers are fabricated using several deposition techniques including RF and DC magnatron sputtering, thermal evaporation and liquid based deposition techniques: chemical bath deposition and spin-coating. Films and devices are measured and characterized by SEM, EDS, XRD, Raman, UV-Vis, Hall, Light and Dark J-V, and EQE.

Latest Update

Naval Research Laboratory

In collaboration with the NRL Optical Sciences Division Researchers Dr. Jason Myers and Dr. Jesse Frantz, Hodges is proposing a collaborative research project in the development of low cost, efficient thin film CZTSSe solar cells on flexible substrates. This group was recognized for their successful demonstration of flexible PV devices sputtered in a single processing step.

UTEP-UCSB NSF PREM

Hodges was recently accepted into the NSF Partnership for Research and Education in Materials (PREM) program directed by Dr. Luis Echegoyen, Robert A. Welch Professor of Chemistry. This is a joint program between UTEP and the University of California at Santa Barbara. Two PREM students are part of Hodges' research group: Jose Galindo and Donato Kava, working with Perovskites and CZTS thin film solar cells.
