Materials and Devices

PV materials are synthesized and devices are fabricated using several deposition techniques including RF and DC magnatron sputtering, thermal evaporation and liquid based deposition techniques: chemical bath deposition and spin-coating. Thin films and devices are measured and characterized by SEM, EDS, XRD, Raman, UV-Vis, Hall, 4 point probe, light and dark J-V, and EQE. Additional tools are used at the University of Texas at Austin Microelectronics Research Center (MRC), part of the NSF National Nanotechnology Infrastructure Network (NNIN).

Thin Film PV Research Group

The Thin Film PV research group is a diverse group of research students working with and sythesizing different organic and inorganic PV materials and devices. Current research materials include methylammonium lead tri-iodide CH3NH3PbI3 perovskites and copper zinc tin sulfide/seledide Cu2ZnSn(S,Se)4 absorbers, spiro-OMeTAD, P3HT, ZnO,ZnO:Al, ITO, TiO2, Mo, Ag, Al, and Au. Materials are deposited using different deposition techniques, ranging from spin coating, chemical bath deposition, dip coating, rf and dc sputtering, and thermal evaporation. Materials are characterized and studied using several tools, and techniques including SEM, XRD, UV-Vis, Raman, Hall, and 4 point probe. Subrate and superstrate PV devices are designed, fabricated, characterized and tested. Light and dark JV measurements are performed, followed by external quantum efficiency/sprectral response.
